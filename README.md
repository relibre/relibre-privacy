# Privacy Policy — RE:LIBRE

**Last updated: March 10, 2026**

RE:LIBRE is a free mobile application that helps formerly incarcerated people, folks recovering from drug or alcohol addiction, and refugees find resources, listen to guided audio sessions and watch practical videos. We care about your privacy. This page explains in clear, simple language what information we handle and how we protect you.

---

## 1. What we collect — and what we don't

- **Access code only.** You open the app with a code that your partner organization gives you. We ask for no email address, no name, no password.
- **No personal details.** We do not collect or store your nationality, immigration status, criminal record, addiction history or any other identifying information.
- **No location data.** The app does not track where you are or where you have been.
- **No analytics tied to you.** We don't link audio/video plays to your identity. The only things we log are generic errors and performance data needed to keep the app working.

---

## 2. How we use data

Because we collect so little, there is very little we can use.

- Store and stream the audio and video files you play so the app works smoothly.
- Record anonymous crash reports to fix bugs.
- Remember your access code so you can open the app again without typing it each time.

---

## 3. Third-party services we work with

- **Supabase.** Audio and video content lives on Supabase cloud storage. We do not share any personal data with Supabase; they only serve files when the app asks for them.
- **Expo / React Native.** We build the app using Expo and React Native. Expo may collect basic anonymous usage statistics, but we do not send it any user data.
- **Apple App Store and Google Play.** Your download and update information is handled by Apple or Google; we do not receive that data.

---

## 4. Data security

We take reasonable technical measures to protect the small amount of information we have. Audio/video files are stored securely in Supabase with restricted access. The app does not transmit personal data over the network. Nevertheless, no system is 100% safe, so please avoid sharing sensitive information inside the app that isn't required.

---

## 5. Children's privacy

RE:LIBRE is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any data from children under 13. If a parent believes their child has used the app, please contact us and we will delete the code and any related records.

---

## 6. Your rights

If you live in the European Union or in other places with similar laws (like the UK), you have the right to:

- Ask what data we have about you.
- Ask us to delete anything we hold about you.
- Withdraw any consent you gave.
- Complain to a data protection authority if you think we are breaking the law.

To exercise your rights, please email [info@reLibre.app](mailto:info@reLibre.app).

---

## 7. Sensitive information

We know some of our users are in vulnerable situations. We **do not** store or share any details that could identify your criminal history, addiction status, immigration status, or any other sensitive trait. The access codes are random strings that are not linked to your identity.

---

## 8. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy if the app changes. When we do, we will post the new version here and change the "Last updated" date. Continued use of the app after a change means you accept the new policy.
